4. Accountability Deposits

The core mechanism of MAST is the accountability deposit: a sum of money that you voluntarily allocate from your available balance to back a specific personal goal. This deposit is held for the duration of your commitment and is returned to your available balance upon successful completion of the goal.

When you make an accountability deposit, you are making a commitment to yourself. You define the goal, set the deadline, choose the amount, and select the strictness level. The AI agent assists in structuring these commitments but does not impose goals or stakes on you.

Accountability deposits are not bets, wagers, or speculative instruments. No other party stands to profit from your success or failure. There is no counterparty, no odds, and no element of chance. The outcome is entirely within your control and determined solely by whether you complete the goal you set for yourself.

5. Strictness Levels

When creating a commitment, you choose a strictness level that determines how binding your accountability deposit is. Strictness levels range from flexible to iron:

  • Flexible — You may cancel the commitment and reclaim your deposit before the deadline with minimal restrictions
  • Moderate — Cancellation is possible but may be subject to conditions or a cooling-off period
  • Firm — Cancellation is restricted; deposits are returned only upon verified completion
  • Iron — No cancellation, no exceptions. The deposit is returned only if the goal is completed before the deadline

You acknowledge that by selecting a higher strictness level, you are voluntarily limiting your ability to recover your deposit without completing the goal. Choose a level that matches your personal accountability needs.

6. Forfeiture

If a commitment deadline passes without the goal being marked as completed, the accountability deposit is forfeited in accordance with the terms you agreed to when creating the commitment. Forfeiture is an automatic consequence of the commitment structure you chose.

By creating an accountability deposit, you acknowledge and accept that forfeiture is a possible outcome and that it is the direct result of your own voluntary decision to create a financially backed commitment. MAST is not responsible for returning forfeited deposits.
